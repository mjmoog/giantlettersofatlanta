The History of Marquee Letters

Giant Letters of Atlanta

Marquee letters have become one of the most sought-after decorative elements for weddings, birthdays, corporate events, and celebrations of all kinds. But where did the idea of giant illuminated letters come from? The story stretches back more than a century, rooted in the dazzling world of theater and entertainment. At Giant Letters of Atlanta, we love sharing the rich history behind the letters we bring to your events every week.

The Birth of the Marquee Sign

The word "marquee" originally referred to the large canopy or awning that extended over the entrance of a theater or hotel. By the late 1800s, theater owners in New York, Chicago, and other major cities began adding illuminated signs to these canopies to attract audiences. Early marquee signs used gas lighting, but the invention of the incandescent light bulb by Thomas Edison in 1879 changed everything. Suddenly, theater facades could be lined with rows of bright bulbs that spelled out show names and showtimes. Broadway became synonymous with brilliant, twinkling marquee signs, and the trend quickly spread to movie palaces across the country.

From Theaters to Hollywood and Beyond

As the film industry boomed in the early twentieth century, marquee letters became even more iconic. Movie theaters across America competed to have the most eye-catching signage, with bold, bulb-studded letters announcing the latest pictures. The classic marquee aesthetic, with its warm Edison-style bulbs arranged around each letter, became a symbol of glamour and excitement. Las Vegas took the concept to a new level in the mid-1900s, filling entire streets with enormous illuminated signs that defined the city's identity.

The Modern Marquee Letter Revival

By the early 2000s, marquee letters experienced a renaissance in the world of interior design and event decor. Designers began creating freestanding, oversized letters inspired by vintage theater signage. These letters brought the same sense of drama and elegance to private events that marquees had brought to public spaces for over a century. Today, giant marquee letters are a staple at weddings, milestone birthdays, brand activations, and holiday celebrations. Modern versions use energy-efficient LED bulbs that produce the same warm glow with far less heat and power consumption.

Marquee Letters in Atlanta Today

Atlanta has embraced the marquee letter trend wholeheartedly. From elegant weddings at the Biltmore Ballrooms to lively birthday celebrations in East Atlanta, illuminated letters add a timeless touch to events across the city. At Giant Letters of Atlanta, we are proud to be part of this tradition. Our letters honor the classic marquee aesthetic while incorporating modern materials and lighting technology. Every time we set up a display, we are connecting our clients to a century-old tradition of using light and letters to create unforgettable moments.
